TEAR

                          BY

  •     Linda

        Hogan

      1. It was the time before
      2.   I was born.
      3.     I was thin.
      4.       I was hungry. I was
      5.         Only a restlessness inside a womanâ€™s body.

      6.         Above us, lightning split open the sky.
      7.           Below us, wagon wheels cut land in two.
      8.             Around us were the soldiers,
      9.               young and afraid,
      10.                 who did not trust us
      11.                   with scissors or knives
      12.                     but with needles.

      13.                     Tear dresses they were called
      14.                     because settler cotton was torn
      15.                     in straight lines
      16.                     like the roads we had to follow
      17.                     to Oklahoma.

      18.                   But when the cloth was torn,
      19.                 it was like tears,
      20.               impossible to hold back,
      21.             and so they were called
      22.           by this other name,
      23.         for our weeping.

      24.         I remember the women.
      25.         Tonight they walk
      26.         out from the shadows
      27.         with black dogs,
      28.         children, the dark heavy horses,
      29.         and worn-out men.

      30.         They walk inside me. This blood
      31.           is a map of the road between us.
      32.             I am why they survived.
      33.               The world behind them did not close.
      34.                 The world before them is still open.
      35.                   All around me are my ancestors,
      36.                     my unborn children.
      37.                       I am the tear between them
      38.                         and both sides live.
